Kurumi Soba

Description

Kurumi Soba stands as an elegant evolution of traditional Japanese buckwheat noodles, where the nutty depth of walnuts (kurumi) transforms the classic dish into something extraordinary. The foundation rests on meticulously crafted soba noodles, which offer a distinctive texture that perfectly balances tenderness and chew. These noodles bathe in a velvety sauce that marries the rich, earthy essence of toasted walnuts with delicate umami notes, creating a harmonious blend that envelops each strand.

The dish comes adorned with precisely cut green onions, providing a sharp, fresh contrast to the creamy walnut sauce. Pristine mounds of grated daikon radish contribute a crisp, peppery brightness that cuts through the richness, while scattered crushed walnuts introduce a delightful crunch that echoes the sauce's nutty profile. Optional touches of wasabi or shichimi togarashi allow diners to personalize their experience with varying levels of heat.

When served cold, as is traditional during Japan's sweltering summers, the temperature elevates the clean, distinct flavors of each component while making the dish remarkably refreshing. This sophisticated interplay of textures and tastes exemplifies the Japanese culinary principle of balanced simplicity.

Ingredients

Mori Tsuyu:

  • 2 tablespoons (30 milliliters) mirin
  • 1/4 cup (50 grams) granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup (120 milliliters) soy sauce
  • 1 1/3 cups (315 milliliters) dashi

Soba:

  • 1 pound (450 grams) dried soba noodles
  • 1/2 cup (60 grams) walnuts
  • 2 tablespoons thinly sliced green onions
